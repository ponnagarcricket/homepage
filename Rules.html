1 THE PLAYERS

1.1 Number of players

A match should be played between two sides with equal number of players on each side.

1.2 Common player

In some cases, the total number of players may be odd. In such case, the extra player can be act as a common player to both the teams when both team agrees

1.2.1 Common Fielder

The extra player can be assigned to field for both the teams. He can act as both bowler and batsman.

1.2.2 Common Batsman

The extra player can be act only as the batsman for both the teams. He can neither bowl nor field.

1.2.3 Common player priority

The ownership of common player always goes to batting team. They can call the common at any time during the match to act as their side batsman. When the common is not batting, the ownership will be transfered to bowling team.

1.2.4 Change of common

When any player leaves the match for any reason, the common can be shifted to that particular team.

2 THE FORMAT

2.1 Choosing the format

A format from given list or a new format can be chosen when both teams agree.

2.2 Changing the format

Changing the format during the match should be avoided. In some cases, When both the teams agree, the format can get changed during the course of match.

3 THE WICKETS

3.1 Batting wicket

The match cannot be started without the batting wicket. In some cases, the bails in batting wicket can be avoided.

3.1.1 Uniformity

The batting stumps should be in same length, width and colour. Each should be kept at equal distance.

3.2 Bowling wicket

The bowling wicket can be stumps or a single long stone. The bails in bowling stump is always considered as dead.

4 THE LEGAL RUNS

4.1 Running between wickets

when the ball is in ACTIVE AREA(5.1), the RUNNER(4.2) is allowed to run as many as possible runs before OVER THROW(4.3).

4.2 The Runner

By default, the batsman will be active runner.

4.2.1 By-Runner

When the batsman wants a by-ruuner, any player from the batting team can be act as a by-runner. The common player cannot act s by-runner.

4.3 Over throw

When any fielder throws the ball and it crosses the stumps, it is considered as over throw.

4.3.1 Unclaimable runs

The runs by runner after over throw is unclaimable.

4.3.2 Claimable run

When the runner left his wicket area before the ball get crosed over stump can be claimable. Even though, the fielder throws the ball, it wont be considered as unclaimable, if the batsman leaves his wicket area.

4.4 Boundaries

When the ball croses the line of boundary without any contact in active area, six run will be rewarded and in contact four run will be given.

5 NOT RUN

5.1 Active area

The two poles on both off and leg are the starting point of active area.

5.1.1 Off active

In off side, the active area starts from pole and ends at boundary. The fence area in off side is considered as inactive. But limited runs(5.4) can be awarded.

5.1.2 Leg active

The leg active area starts from leg pole to the great puthu of ponnagar. To mark boundary in leg, a stone marking is provided. But only straight boundary will be considered for six and fours.

5.2 Behind the pole

If the ball is behind the active poles, it will be considered as not run.

5.3 Hitting the pole

When the ball hits the pole, it will be considered as active area.

5.4 Limited runs

In some cases the ball travels in both active and unactive area or it will be directly goes into unactive through active area. For those cases, the limited run concept will be suitable.

5.4.1 Deviated ball

When the ball hits the active area and moves towards unactive area by itself will be given only one run.

5.4.2 Contact with fielder

When the ball get contact with the fielder and goes into unactive area, the maximum claimable runs will be three.

6 WIDE DELIVERY

6.1 The conventional wide

When the ball goes away from wide limit on both sides and above the head of batsman, it will be considered as wide delivery.

6.2 Extra runs

The bowler will be allowed to bowl two wides in an over without any extras. But when the bowler bowls more than two wides, the extra one run will be given to the batting team for each wide.

6.3 Exceptions

when the ball hits any part of the batsman, bat and goes above the wicket stumps will not be considered as wide.

7 THE REBALL

7.1 Reball for wide

In an over, always a wide ball should be compensate with a reball.

7.2 No ball

When the ball directly goes above the hip level of batsman or the bowler's leg is outside the bowling wicket line, it will be considered as no ball. No extra run will be provided for no ball. But the runs scored for that ball will be claimable. A reball should be also bowled.

7.3 Dead ball

When the ball pitched more than once outside the batting wicket area, it will be considred as dead ball. The same rules of a no ball will be applied.

7.4 More than one bounce

A bowler is allowed to bowl only one bounce in an over. if he bowls more than one, it will be considered as no ball.

7.5 Unaware batsman

When the batsman is unaware of the ball, it will be given a reball.

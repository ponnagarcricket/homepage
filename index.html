Started with Limitations

In early stages, the batsmen were allowed to hit the ball towards deep extra cover, extra cover, mid off, long off, mid on and long on to get legal runs.

Small actions can bring
HUGE changes

One fine rainy day made the ground unplayable. But the players didnt gave up. They cleared the obstacles in leg side. So batsmen were allowed to hit towards leg.

Slow and Steady
wins the race

The small amount of works here and there sum up to make a great difference. Finally the ground is ready to allow the batsmen to hit towards both leg and off.

Legal Formats

Due to players availablity, climatical contidions, time constrains and several other factors, there are some formats are designed.
When both bowling and batting team agrees, one of the format will be chosen.

  • T-10 (10 overs)

    The longest limited overs format. Mostly this format is preferable when both teams have more players and more time.

  • E-8 (8 overs)

    The most played and prefered format. The 80% of matches will be in this format.

  • S-4 (7 to 4 overs)

    When any team getting confusion in choosing a format, randomly they choose between 7 to 4.

  • T-2 (2 overs)

    When two or three other format matches get finished, this 2 over firmat will be chosen.

  • 1 on 1 (1 over)

    The revenge format.when everything going odd, this format will be chosen to take revenge.

  • The Test

    Less preferable format of all. Only less number of games have been played in this format.
